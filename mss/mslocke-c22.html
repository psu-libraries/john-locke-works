Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 22

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from Gabriel Towerson, James Tyrrell, and others.

189 leaves.

Individual items in the volume include:

Towerson, Gabriel, 1635?-1697.   [4 letters, 1660-61]

ff. 1-2:

Letter from Towerson, 23 October 1660.

Salutation: Mr. Lock,

Signature: Your most affectionate freind and servant | G: Towerson | All-Soules. October 23. 1660.

Address: For his much respected freind Mr. Joh: Lock Jun: at Pensford | Leave this at Mr. Tho: Days in St. Tho: street Bristoll

Printed: Corr., i. 104.

ff. 3-4:

Letter from Towerson, [ca. 3 November 1660?]

Salutation: Mr. Lock,

Signature: Your most affectionate freind and servant | G: Towerson

Address: For his most respected freind Mr. John Lock Jun. at Pensford | Leave this at Mr. Thomas Day’s in St. Thomas street Bristoll

Printed: Corr., i. 106; Von Leyden (ed.), Essays on the law of nature, pages 8-9; Goldie (ed.), Selected correspondence, pages 21-22.

ff. 5-6:

Letter from Towerson, 12 March 1660[/61].

Salutation: Mr. Lock,

Signature: Your most affectionate freind and servant | G. Towerson | Oxon. March: 12. 1660.

Printed: De Beer i. 115.

Answered by Locke on 15 March (missing).

ff. 7-8:

Letter from Towerson, 9 April 1661.

Superscription: All-Soules. Apr. 9. 1661.

Salutation: Mr. Lock

Signature: G: Towerson

Address: For his much respected freind Mr. John Locke | Leave this at Mr. Tho: Dayes in St Thomas street Bristoll

Answers Locke’s letter of 15 March (missing).

Printed: De Beer i. 118.
