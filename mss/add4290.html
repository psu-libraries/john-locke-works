British Library

Add. MS. 4290

A bound volume of miscellaneous papers relating to Locke, collected by Thomas Birch.

Includes:

ff. 1-2:

Copy of a letter from Locke to Anthony Collins, 23 August 1704, “To be deliver’d to him after my decease”; original in The Carl H. Pforzheimer Library, New York.

[printed from the copy in Bourne, Life, vol. 2:550-551; part printed in A collection of several pieces (1720), pp. 328-329; De Beer, viii. 3648; Goldie (ed.), Selected correspondence, pp. 334-335; passage printed in postscript to J. Le Clerc, An account of the life and writings of John Locke, 3rd ed. (1714), p. 28]

ff. 11-14:

Some thoughts concerning reading and study for a gentleman (1703)

[printed in A collection of several pieces (1720), pp. 276-277; De Beer, vii. 3384]

[printed in A collection of several pieces (1720), pp. 281-282; De Beer, viii. 3449]
