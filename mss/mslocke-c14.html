Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 14

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from the Dutch theologian Philippus van Limborch, various members of the Locke family, and others.

iv, 233 leaves.

Unknown correspondents   [2 letters, 1650s]

f. 165:

Letter between unknown correspondents, [late May 1659?].

Signature: Sir

Signature: yours sine fuco aut fraude | S L

Appears to have been written from London or Oxford to Pensford. See also letter no. 46, which also uses the phrase “since fuco aut fraude”.

Printed: Corr., i. (56).

f. 166:

Letter between unknown correspondents, 24 January 1659.

Not in Locke’s hand; Locke is unlikely to be either the author or the recipient; perhaps written by an amanuensis for Locke’s father. Appears to have been written by an Oxonian to someone in London. See also letter no. 56, which also uses the phrase “since fuco aut fraude”.

Signature: Sir | your unfeigned freind and servant yours sine fuco aut fraude | S L | 24 Jan: 58[/59]

Address: To his

Printed: Corr., i. (46).

Locke, John, sen., 1606-1661.   [2 letters, 1659]

ff. 167-168:

Letter from John Locke, sen., 21 May 1659.

Signature: I am yours | J L | Saterday 21 May 59

Address: To Mr John Locke att his Chamber in Ch: Ch: Oxon this.

Answers a letter dated 17 May (lost); answered by Locke on 22 June. The inner pages contain Locke’s pencil drafts of Letter no. 64 and Letter no. 65.

Printed: Corr., i. 54.

f. 169:

Letter from John Locke, sen. to an unknown correspondent, [summer 1656?].

Locke, Peter, 1607-1686   [10 letters, 1666-1683]

f. 182:

Letter from Locke, 16 August 1666.

Salutation: Lovinge Cosen

Signature: Your Lovinge unkill | Pe: Locke | … | Sutton this 16th August 1666

Address: For mr John Locke att Ch: Ch: Colledge In Oxford [added in another hand] To be left with Mr. David Thomas or Mr. Nathanie[l] Hodges at Astrop.

The writer was the younger brother of Locke’s father and acted as Locke’s agent for his lands in Somerset.

Printed: Corr., i. 206.
