Gloriatio   [May?] 1688

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke c. 33, f. 23v.

Description:   A note with the caption “Gloriatio” and initialed by Locke. Undated but occurs on a sheet dated May 1688.

Publications:   None

Tolerantia pro   [after May 1688]

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke c. 33, f. 24r.

Description:   A list of scriptural passages headed “Tolerantia pro” (i.e., passages that support religious toleration). Goldie gives it the title “Scriptures for toleration”. The sheet is dated “May 1688”.

Publications:  

  1. A letter concerning toleration and other writings / John Locke ; edited and with an introduction by Mark Goldie. – Indianapolis : Liberty Fund, ©2010. – p. 189 [Locke #67B].

Discussions:   Goldie, publication #1, p. xl.

Caballa 88   (1688)

Location:   Bodleian Library, Locke’ Library, H&L 558 [inserted]

Description:   A paper in Locke’s hand with the title “Caballa [16]88”. It consists of a single sheet of paper, consisting of notes on Chapter 3 of Christian Knorr von Rosenroth’s Adumbratio Kabbalae Christianae, which was issued in 1684 with the second volume of Knorr’s collection of Kabbalistic texts, Kabbala Denudata.

Publications:

  1. Christianity, antiquity, and enlightenment : interpretations of Locke / Victor Nuovo. – Dordrecht ; London ; New York : Springer, ©2011. – xix, 274 p. – (Archives internationales d’histoire des idées = International archives of the history of ideas ; 203). – p. 161.

Discussions:   Nuovo, Christianity, antiquity, and enlightenment (2011), p. 133.

Lexicon Syncatabasis 7 88   (1688)

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke c. 27, f. 79-80.

Description:   A paper in Locke’s hand with the title “Lexicon Syncatabasis 7 [16]88”. It consists of a single sheet of paper folded once to form four pages. The inner pages (ff. 79v, 80r) are organized with an alphabet across the top of the opening; Locke’s notes are captioned and indented under the appropriate letter.

The notes consist of definitions of terms taken from Christian Knorr von Rosenroth’s Adumbratio Kabbalae Christianae, which was issued in 1684 with the second volume of Knorr’s collection of Kabbalistic texts, Kabbala Denudata. Although the “7” in the title seems to refer to Chapter 7 of the Adumbratio, the citations in fact cover the entire work.

Publications:

  1. Christianity, antiquity, and enlightenment : interpretations of Locke / Victor Nuovo. – Dordrecht ; London ; New York : Springer, ©2011. – xix, 274 p. – (Archives internationales d’histoire des idées = International archives of the history of ideas ; 203). – p. 161.

Discussions:   Nuovo, Christianity, antiquity, and enlightenment (2011), pp. 132-133.

Pacifick Christians   (1688)

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke c. 27, f. 80a-b.

Description:   A paper in Locke’s hand, with the title “Pacifick Christians” and endorsed by Locke “Pacifici 88.”

On a single sheet folded to form four pages, 236 × 180 mm; f. 80bv is blank, except for the endorsement.
