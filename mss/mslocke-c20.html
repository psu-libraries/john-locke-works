Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 20

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from David Thomas, Matthew Tindal, and others.

210 leaves.

ff. 175-176:

Letter from Tilly, 11 September 1655.

The letter answers one from Locke, probably that drafted in letter #22. Written on f. 175r; on f 175v is a draft of Locke’s answer; on f. 176 is a note by Locke on a cure “For the spleen”.

Signature: Sir | Your friend and servant | Samuel Tilly

Subscription: Oxon: Sep: 11 —55

Address: These For mr John Lock at his father’s house in Pensford. To be left at Mr Thomas Day’s in st Thomas street in Bristoll to be Convey’d as abovesaid.

Endorsement: Mr Tillys letter & answer

Printed: Corr., i. 23.

f. 175v:

Letter to [Tilly?], [late September 1655?].

Draft, written on the verso of the previous letter; answers Tilly’s letter of 11 September.

Printed: Corr., i. 24.
