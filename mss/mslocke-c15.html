227 leaves.

Mandey, Robert.   [3 letters, 1662]

ff. 198-199:

Letter from Mandey, 30 June 1662.

Mandey was steward or secretary to Lord Berkeley; Durdens, near Epsom, was Berkeley’s residence; the letter concerns Berkeley’s son Charles, who was Locke’s pupil at Christ Church.

Salutation: Sir

Signature: Sir | your very humble servant | Rob. Mandey

Subscription: Dardens. June 30. 1662

Address: Theis For mr John Locke Student in Christchurch in Oxford

Printed: De Beer, i. 133.

ff. 200-201:

Letter from Mandey, 19 August 1662.

Salutation: Sir

Signature: Sir | your most obliged and thankfull freind to serve you | Rob: Mandey

Subscripton: Durdens. Aug: 19. 1662

Address: For my much honoured freind mr John Locke Student in Christs-Church, in Oxford. | To the Posthouse

Printed: De Beer, i. 142.

ff. 202-203:

Letter from Mandey, 22 August 1662, with a note from Andrew Crooke, 28 August 1662.

Salutation: Sir,

Signature: Sir | your very Servant | Rob: Mandey

Subscription: Durdens. Aug. 22. 1662.

Signature (postscript): Andrew Crooke

Subscription (postscript): Ag 28. 1662.

Address: (written by Mandey): For my much honour’s freind mr John Locke Student in Christs-Church in Oxford

Printed: De Beer, i. 143.
