Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 8

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from Locke’s friends Richard and Isabella Duke, Andrew Fletcher of Saltoun, John Freke, and others.

244 leaves.

Parry, Elinor, later Mrs. Hawkshaw   [6 letters, 1666-1669]

f. 62:

Letter from [Elinor Parry?], 13 February 1666.

Superscription: The Oxford Gazett: feb: the 13

Signature: Ilia: | Ap: the 10th

The identity of the writer based on the handwriting.

Printed: De Beer, i. 185.

f. 63:

Letter from [Elinor Parry?], 10 April 1666.

Signature: Ilia: | Ap: the 10th

Addres: For mr John Locke at Pensford Leave it as mr Thomas Day’s in st Thomas street: Bristoll if not Call’d for in a weekes time lett it be sent back to Ox ford to mr Lichfild in Catt Street.

The writer is either Scribelia [Elinor Parry] or a close associate of hers; the year is based on the contents of the letter.

Printed: De Beer, i. 193.

f. 64:

Letter from [Elinor Parry?], [19 February 1667?].

Salutation: Deare Sir

Addres: [torn] … Oxford pd to London

Postmark: MR 23

Endorsements: Sc: 66; S 66

Part of leaf torn away. Suggested date from mention of Shrove Tuesday. Answers Locke’s letter of ca. 15 December 1666.

Printed: De Beer, i. 222.

f. 65:

Letter from [Elinor Parry?], [spring 1667?].

Salutation: my deare A[tticus]:

Signature: yours S[cribelia]:

Endorsement: Scribelia 67

Defective; strip torn off side. Date based on Locke’s endorsement and the contents of the letter.

Printed: De Beer, i. 225.

f. 66:

Letter from [Elinor Parry?], 11 April [1669].

Superscription: Ap: the 11th

Salutation: my dear A[tticus]

Signature: yours S[cribelia]:

Address: These For mr Locke at little Exitor Howse on the Strand London

Postmark: AP 26

Endorsement: Scr 11o Apr. 69.

Year from Locke’s endorsement.

Printed: De Beer, i. 232]

f. 67:

Letter from [Elinor Parry?], 25 November [1666?].

Superscription: Ap: the 11th

Salutation: Deare Sir

Signature: Sir | Your most obliged servant Tho: Willson | … | No the 25

Address: for mr John Locke att Christ Church in his absenc to be leaft with mr Thomas at new College Oxon paid to Lo.

Postmark: DE 5

The letter is rendered obscure by attempts to conceal the identity and whereabouts of the sender. The identity of the writer is based on the handwriting and seal; year based on the contents of the letter.

Printed: De Beer, i. 214.
