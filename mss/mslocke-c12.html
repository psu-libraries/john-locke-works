Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 12

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from Henri Justel, Locke’s cousin Peter King, and others.

276 leaves.

Individual items in the volume include:

Ivye, Dr. Ayliffe   [2 letters, 1656?, 1660]

ff. 1-2:

Letter from Dr. Ivye, 20 May 1660.

Salutation: Worthy Sir,

Signature: Your most humble servant | Ayliffe Ivye. | Bristleton may the 20 1660.

Address: For his honoured friend Mr John Lock att his Chamber in Peckwater quadrangle in Christchurch College Oxon

Contains the first mention of Robert Boyle in Locke’s correspondence.

Printed: Corr., i. 97.

f. 3:

Letter from [Dr. Ivye to a German doctor], signed by Ivye.

f. 4:

A version in Locke’s hand of f. 3.

ff. 5-6:

Another version of the same letter, written by Locke in Latin.

f. 7:

Another version of the same letter, written by Locke in Latin.

f. 8:

Letter from [Dr. Ivye to a German doctor], [summer 1656?].

Other versions of this letter, in Latin and English, in Locke’s hand and in (presumably) Ivye’s, exist on ff. 3-7.

Printed: Corr., i. (26).
