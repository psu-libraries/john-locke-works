Atramentum   [1673-74]

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke d. 9, p. 222.

Description:   A note in Locke’s hand, with the marginal caption “Atramentum”. The note is not dated or initialed by Locke, but was probably written by him in 1673 or 1674.

Publications:   None

The early history of the Shaftesbury family

Location:   British Library, Add. MS. 4223, f. 196;
copies in Hampshire Record Office, Malmesbury Papers, 9M73/G233;
National Archives, PRO 30/24/16, ff. 144-145;
National Archives, PRO 30/24/17, f. 285.

Description:   A brief untitled account of the early history of the Shaftesbury family. The endorsement on Locke’s autograph includes the date “73”; based on this and internal evidence, it is likely that it was written between November 1673 and March 1674. Four manuscripts have survived; in addition to the autograph in the British Library, there is a copy in the Malmesbury papers and two copies in the Public Record Office.

The British Library manuscript is a single leaf measuring approximately 304 &timres; 227 mm. The text is entirely in Locke’s hand, and is endorsed “Shaftesbury E of places & Extraction 73”.

The manuscript in the Malmesbury papers is a single leaf measuring approximately 320 &ties; 210 mm. It is an undated copy in an unknown hand; it was probably copied from Locke’s autograph, since the texts are nearly identical.

The manuscript in bundle 16 of the Shaftesbury papers is written on the first leaf of a bifolium, each leaf measuirng 227 × 187 mm, and endorsed in a later hand “Account of the Ashley Family”. It is undated and in an unknown hand. The text indicates that it was derived from the Malmesbury manuscript.

The manuscript in bundle 17 of the Shaftesbury papers is written on the first page of a bifolium, each leaf measuring 292 × 206 mm, and endorsed “May 1677 | A Copy of Mr Fisher’s paper concerning the Earle of Shaftesbury”.

Publications:

  1. Literary and historical writings / John Locke ; edited by J. R. Milton. – Oxford : Oxford University Press, 2019. – (The Clarendon edition of the works of John Locke). – Pages 360-361.

Discussions:   Milton, publication #1 above, page 141-142, 174-177.
