Allestry, James, d. 1670.   [1 letter, 1661]

f. 21:

Letter from James Allestry to Samuel Tilly, 14 May 1661.

Tilly left a manuscript with Allestry, a London bookseller, which Allestry is anxious to publish; De Beer argues (contra Von Leyden and Abrams) that this is not likely to be Locke’s Second Tract on Government.

Superscription: London May 14 1661

Salutation: Worthy Sir,

Signature: Your respectfull freind and servant | James Allestry.

Address: To his much Esteemed friend Mr. Samuel Tilly at Mr. Prats neare Carfax in Oxford, These

Printed: Abrams (ed.), Two tracts on government (1967), p. 244; De Beer, i. (123).
