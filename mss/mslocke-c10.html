Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 10

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from Denis Grenville, Anna Grigg, and others.

254 leaves.

Grenville, Dr. Denis, 1637-1703   [23 letters, 1677-79]

ff. 62-63:

Letter from Grenville, [12/22? March 1677].

Superscription: Munday Morning 7. clock

Salutation: Deare Sir

Signature: Sir | Your true and affectionate Freind | De. Grenville.

Endorsement: Dr Grenville Mar. 77

Printed: Corr., i. 329]

ff. 64-65:

Letter from Grenville, [ca. 3/13-5/15 March 1677].

Salutation: Sir

Signature: Deare Sir | Your most unworthy FREIND, but faithfull and affectionate humble servant | De. Grenville

Address: A Monsieur Monsieur Lock

Endorsement: D: Grenvill Mar. 77

Printed: Corr., i. 326.

ff. 66-67:

Letter from Grenville, [ca. 6/16-8/18 March 1677].

Endorsement: D Grenville Mar. 77 Recreation

In response to this letter, Locke sent Grenville a paper on Recreation [ca. 9/19-11/21 March 1677] and began to draft on essay on Study.

Printed: Corr., i. 327; Goldie (ed.), Selected correspondence, pages 57-58.

ff. 68-69:

Letter from Grenville, [9/]19 November 1677.

Superscription: Aix Nov: 19th 1677.

Salutation: Sir

Signature: Sir | Your most faithfull and affectionate Freind, and humble servant | De Grenville

Endorsement: Dr Grenvil 19 Nov. 77

Printed: Corr., i. 357.

ff. 70-71:

Letter from Grenville, [29 December 1677/]8 January 1678.

Superscription: Aix Jan. 8th. 1677/8.

Salutation: Deare Sir

Signature: Sir | Your faithfull freind and most humble servant | De. Grenville

Address: A Monsieur Monsieur Lock Gentilhomme anglois chez Monsr. Charas Apothecaire Rue de Boucherie Fauxbourg St. Germain A Paris

Endorsement: Mr Grenvill 8 Jan. 78

Answers a lost letter from Locke dated “the 22th of last month”.

Printed: Corr., i. 366.

ff. 72-79:

Letter from Grenville, [2/]12 March 1678.

Superscription: Aix March 12th. 1677/8.

Salutation: Deare Friend

Signature: Sir | Your Faithfull freind and humble servant | De. Grenville

Endorsements (on the letter and each ennclosure): Mr Grenville 12 Mar. 77/8

With three enclosures: “2. About Temporall Buisnesse” (endorsed ‘Businesse”); “3. Concerning study” (endorsed “Study”); “4. Concerning Conversation” (endorsed “Conversation”). Note: the first paper was Recreation, in Grenville’s letter of ca. 6/16 March 1677.

Answered by Locke on 13/23 March 1678.

Printed: Corr., i. 372.
