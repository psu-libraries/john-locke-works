Bodleian Library, Oxford University

MS. Locke c. 19

A guardbook in which are mounted letters to Locke from Locke’s steward William Stratton, Thomas and Jane Stringer, Dr. Thomas Sydenham, Archbishop Tenison, and others.

185 leaves.

ff. 133-134:

Letter from Stringer, 16 August 1677.

Superscription: Thanett House August 16th. 1677.

Salutation: Deare Sir

Signature: Dearest Sir | Your most affectionate and faithfull servant | T:S:

Address: A monsieur monsieur Lock Gentilhome Anglois Chez monsr. Charas Apothecaire rüe de Boucherie Fauxbourg St Germaine a Paris

Endorsement: Mr Stringer 16. Aug 77

Includes a request from Shaftesbury for “the best mapps of Champaigne, Loraigne, Luxenburge, and the Country between the River Sambre and Luxenburg. because the war in probability will come there againe.” Locke purchased the maps on 20/30 September [Journal 1677, page 279].

Printed: King, Life (1830), 1:70 n.; De Beer, i. 351]

Letter from Sydenham , 4 [June] 1677.

Salutation: Sir,

Signature: Your humble servant and very affectionat friend | Tho: Sydenham | <June> 4o 1677 [damaged]

Address: A Madame Madame Blomer chez son excellence l Embassadeur d Angleterre al Hostel de Turenne á Paris. pour faire tenir a Mr. Locke a Paris

Postmark: IV—

f. 164:

Letter from Sydenham to Mapletoft, 1 December 1677.

Salutation: Dear Doctor

Signature: Your humble servant | Tho: Sydenham | Pell-Mell Dec. 1o. 1677

Endorsements: Dentium Dolor and Dr. Sydenham 1o Dec. 77

Enclosed in Mapletoft’s letter of 3 December; Mapletoft wrote a postscript on the verso, with the address “For yourselfe. Lege solus.”

Printed: De Beer, i. (363), fourth enclosure; Dewhurst, “Sydenham’s letters to John Locke” (1955), pages 317-318; Dewhurst, “A symposium on trigenimal neuralgia” (1957), page 33; Dewhurst, Dr. Thomas Sydenham (1966), pages 169-171.
