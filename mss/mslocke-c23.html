Walls, George, ca. 1645-1727   [15 letters, 1678-1698]

ff. 29-30:

Letter from Walls, 21 September[/1 October] 1678.

Superscription: Christ Church Sept. 21th. =78

Salutation: Sir

Signature: Your Most affectionate servant | George Walls

Address: For Mr John Locke This to be left at Sir John Banks his house in Lyncolns Inne fields London

Postmark: SE 2–

Endorsement: Mr Wall 2[1] Sept. 78

Answers “yours of the 30th of April” and “that from Blois of the 2d of Aug;”.

Printed: Corr., i. 409.

ff. 31-32:

Letter from Walls, 11[?]/21 November [1678].

Superscription: Christ Church Novemb: 11th. <78> [torn]

Salutation: Sir

Signature: Deare Sir. | your most humble servant | George Walls

Address: For Mr John Locke This. To be left at Sir John Banks his house in Lyncoln’s Inne fields London

Postmark: NO 15

Endorsement: Mr Wall 17 Nov. 78

The year is based on Locke’s endorsement; the date is badly written; De Beer accepts “11th.” in spite of Locke’ endorsement.

Printed: Corr., i. 422.

f. 74:

Letter from Westrowe, 30 July [June?] 1660.

De Beer argues from the contents that the letter was not written on July 30; he suggests June instead.

Superscription: London July 30th 1660

Salutation: Honest John,

Signature: Your freind and what more affection can clame | Thomas Westrowe

On the reverse, probably written by Locke:

How hate full is a Locke to some
confineing them within a Roome
But plesant is a Locke to me it
keepes me from bad company

Printed: Corr., i. 99.
