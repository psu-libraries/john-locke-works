Third Reply to the Bishop of Worcester [Presentation copies]   [1697?]

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke c. 25, f. 53v.

Description:   A list in Locke’s hand of persons to receive presentation copies of his Reply to the … Bishop of Worcester’s answer to this second letter, in a column headed “3 Rep to Bp Worcester 1698”, on a sheet endorsed “Copys 95”. The list provides for 41 copies; the Letter was published in mid-November.

The manuscript consists of on folded sheet, 310 × 193 mm; this list is in the third column on the second page.

Publication:

  1. The correspondence of John Locke / edited by E.S. de Beer. – Oxford : Clarendon Press, 1976-89. – (Clarendon edition of the works of John Locke) – vol. 8:453. [list K]
  2. John Locke, a descriptive bibliography / Jean S. Yolton. – Bristol, England : Thoemmes Press, 1998. – pp. 301-302.

Discussions:   De Beer, “Distribution lists for copies of Locke’s books,” publication #1 above; Yolton, publication #2 above, pp. 301-302; Goldie, “Distribution lists for copies of Locke’s books and Boyle’s General history of the air” (2004)

Observations on Mr Bolds papers   (December 1698)

Location:   Bodleian Library, MS. Locke c. 27, ff. 147-150.

Description:   Notes and corrections by Locke, endorsed “Observations on Mr Bolds papers Dec 98”, on a manuscript draft of Samuel Bold, Some considerations on the principal objections and arguments, which have been publish’d against Mr. Locke’s Essay of humane understanding (1699). Bold’s draft is MS. Locke, c. 27, ff. 151-156.

On four sheets of paper; 247 × 110 mm (f. 147), 218 × 156 mm (f. 148), 220 × 157 mm (f. 149), and 185 × 150 mm (f. 150). Locke numbered the first seven pages.

Publications:

  1. [with omissions] “Testi teologico-filosofici lockiani dal MS. Locke c.27 della Lovelace Collection” / a cura di Mario Sina. // IN: Rivista di filosofia neo-scolastic. – 64 (1972):413-416.
  2. The Digital Locke Project / Dr. Paul Schuurman, project director. – Amsterdam : Digital Production Centre of the University Library of the University of Amsterdam, 2006-  . – Normalized and diplomatic texts available.
  3. Of the conduct of the understanding and other writings on philosophy, 1690â€“1704 / John Locke ; edited by Paul Schuurman and J. C. Walmsley. – Oxford : Clarendon Press, in preparation. – (The Clarendon edition of the works of John Locke)

Discussions:   Sina, publication #1 above, pp. 412-413; Schuurman, Digital Locke project, text descriptions [viewed 2008/07/26]
