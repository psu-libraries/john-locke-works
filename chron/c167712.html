Monday, 1 December 1677, n.s.

21 November 1677, o.s.

At Paris

Thursday, 2 December 1677, n.s.

22 November 1677, o.s.

At Paris

Journal entries: Degree, Countesse of Northumberlands case, Dentium dolor cum convulsionibus, Convulsio [repeated on pages 372, 373, 374, 376] — [Dewhurst]

Letter dated: From Dr. John Mapletoft. — [De Beer, i. 358]

Letter (undated): From Mrs. Margaret Blomer. — [De Beer, i. 359]

Between 6 and 7 p.m., Locke received a note from Mrs. Blomer, summonning him to attend on her mistress, the Countess of Northumberland (wife of the English ambassador), who was suffering from a violent toothache, accompanied by a facial tic [“convulsio”] (diagnosed on the basis of Locke’s case notes as trigeminal neuralgia). Locke attended the Countess for the next two weeks and sent letters to Mapletoft asking him to consult with his London medical colleagues. The case is described in Kenneth Dewhurst, “A symposium on trigenimal neuralgia” (1957).
