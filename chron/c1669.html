April

Undated; probably ca. October 1668

Letter from De Briolay de Beaupreau to Mr. Browne.

July

Date uncertain:

Paper by Benjamin Worsley headed “Animadversions on my Lord de Wits paper presented to my Ld Arlington” [Adversaria 1661, pp. 253-262]. Date is that of Worsley’s paper; the date of Locke’s copy is undetermined.

December

Sunday, 26 December 1669, o.s.

5 January 1670, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 9h: “Extream hard Forst.” — [Boyle, 12:78]

“I began to keep a Register of Heat and Cold at London.”

Source: Boyle = The general history of the air, designed and begun by the Honble Robert Boyle Esq; cited from The works of Robert Boyle. Volume 12 (2000), pp. 70-89.
