1669

January | February | March | April | May | June
July | August | September | October | November | December

February

Wednesday, 24 February 1669, o.s.

6 March 1669, n.s.

Letter from Benjamin Worsley to Buckingham, relating to the Jamaican sugar trade [Adversaria 1661, pages 180-214; date is that of Worsley’s letter; the date of Locke’s copy is undetermined].

April

Saturday, 11 April 1669, o.s.

21 April 1669, n.s.

Letter dated: From [Elinor Parry]; year from Locke’s endorsement; addressed to Locke at Exeter House, London. — [De Beer, i. 232]

Date uncertain; April 1669

Letter (undated?): From De Briolay de Beaupreau to Mr. Browne. — [De Beer, i. (231)]

  ▲  

July

Date uncertain:

Paper by Benjamin Worsley headed “Animadversions on my Lord de Wits paper presented to my Ld Arlington” [Adversaria 1661, pp. 253-262; date is that of Worsley’s paper; the date of Locke’s copy is undetermined].

August

Sunday, 29 August 1669, o.s.

8 September 1669, n.s.

Locke’s birthday, age 37.

Letter dated: From Anthony Ashley Cooper, Baron Ashley, Wimborne St. Giles; addressed to Locke at Belvoir Castle. — [De Beer, i. 234]

Locke was at Belvoir Castle, seat of the Earl of Rutland, negotiating a marriage between the future 2nd Earl of Shaftesbury and Lady Dorothy Manners, daughter of the Earl of Rutland; the marriage took place on September 22.

September

Sunday, 6 September 1669, o.s.

16 September 1669, n.s.

Letter dated: From Anthony Ashley Cooper, Baron Ashley; addressed to Locke at Belvoir Castle. — [De Beer, i. 235]

  ▲  

October

Monday, 5 October 1669, o.s.

15 October 1669, n.s.

Letter dated: From Anthony Ashley Cooper, Baron Ashley, London; addressed to Locke at Belvoir Castle. — [De Beer, i. 236]

Monday, 19 October 1669, o.s.

29 October 1669, n.s.

Letter dated: From Dr. David Thomas, Salisbury; addressed to Locke at Exeter House, London. — [De Beer, i. 237]

November

Wednesday, 18 November 1669, o.s.

28 November 1669, n.s.

Letter dated: From Dr. David Thomas; addressed to Locke at Exeter House, London; the date is based on postmark and endorsement. — [De Beer, i. 238]

December

Sunday, 26 December 1669, o.s.

5 January 1670, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 9h: “Extream hard Frost.” — [Boyle, 12:78]

“I began to keep a Register of Heat and Cold at London.”

Date uncertain; December 1669?

Letter (undated?): To [Margaret Beavis?];; fragmentary draft. — [De Beer, i. 239]

Undated

[about 1669?]

Letter dated: From John Cockshutt. — [De Beer, i. 233]
