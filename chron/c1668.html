January

Wednesday, 22 January 1668, o.s.

1 February 1668, n.s.

Locke’s commonplace book “Adversaria 1661” contains copies by Locke of several papers by Benjamin Worsley relating to trade. In October 1673, Locke succeeded Worsley as secretary to the Council for Trade and Foreign Plantations. It was presumably around this time that he made copies of some of his predecessor’s papers.

Copy by Locke of a memorandum on senna cultivation by Benjamin Worsley [Adversaria 1661, pages 263-265; date is that of Worsley’s memorandum].

Person encountered: Benjamin Worsley (manuscript)
