January

Sunday, 3 January 1675, o.s.

13 January 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 16h: “Wind W. Fair.” — [Boyle, 12:80]

April

Wednesday, 14 April 1675, o.s.

24 April 1675, n.s.

Letter dated: From William Allestree. — [De Beer, i. 300]

Sunday, 25 April 1675, o.s.

5 May 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 9h: “Wind NE. Fair.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Monday, 26 April 1675, o.s.

6 May 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 10h: “Wind N. Fair.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Tuesday, 27 April 1675, o.s.

7 May 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 12h: “Wind E. Fair.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Wednesday, 28 April 1675, o.s.

8 May 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 9h: “Wind E. Close.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

  ▲  

May

Wednesday, 2 May 1675, o.s.

12 May 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 9h: “Close.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Sir Patience Ward writes a letter of credit on Locke’s behalf, to Mr. Nicolas Lee, merchant in Nantes, for 20 pounds. — [MS. Locke c. 23, f. 61]

Thursday, 20 May 1675, o.s.

30 May 1675, n.s.

Letter dated: To Henry Oldenburg. — [De Beer, i. 299]

Sunday, 23 May 1675, o.s.

2 June 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 15h: “Cloudy.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Thursday, 20 May 1675, o.s.

6 May 1675, n.s.

Meeting of the Royal Society; Oldenburg reads the description of a poisonous fish found in the Bahama Islands, communicated to him by Locke in his letter of 20 May.

June

Tuesday, 8 June 1675, o.s.

18 June 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 22h: “Cloudy.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Wednesday, 9 June 1675, o.s.

19 June 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 13h: “Close.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Tuesday, 10 June 1675, o.s.

20 June 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 14h: “Close.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Sunday, 13 June 1675, o.s.

23 June 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 10h: “Wind E. Close.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

Monday, 14 June 1675, o.s.

24 June 1675, n.s.

Weather observations at London, 9h: “Fair.” — [Boyle, 12:81]

  ▲  

August

Thursday, 12 August 1676, o.s.

22 August 1676, n.s.

Letter dated: From Richard Lilburne. — [De Beer, i. 300]

Thursday, 19 August 1676, o.s.

29 August 1676, n.s.

Letter dated: From Isaac Rush. — [De Beer, i. 301]

Sunday, 29 August 1676, o.s.

8 September 1676, n.s.

Locke’s birthday, age 43

September

Tuesday, 14 Sep. 1675, o.s.

24 Sep. 1675, n.s.

Letter dated: From W. Fanshawe. — [De Beer, i. 302]

  ▲  

November

Monday, 8 Nov. 1675, o.s.

18 Nov. 1675, n.s.

At London?

Letter dated: From Dr. John Fell. — [De Beer, i. 303]

Thursday, 11 Nov. 1675, o.s.

21 Nov. 1675, n.s.

At London?

Letter dated: From Samuel Thomas. — [De Beer, i. 304]

Friday, 12 Nov. 1675, o.s.

22 Nov. 1675, n.s.

“In the evening I went to Gravesend.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Letter dated: From Dr. Henry Woodward. — [De Beer, i. 305]

Saturday, 13 Nov. 1675, o.s.

23 Nov. 1675, n.s.

Letter dated: From Peter Locke. — [De Beer, i. 306]

Sunday, 14 Nov. 1675, o.s.

24 Nov. 1675, n.s.

“Took water at Dover and that day arrived at Calais.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Locke apparently crossed the Channel with John Lord Berkeley (1607-1678) on his yacht and continued with him to Abbeville; Berkeley was going to France as the new English ambassador. — [DeBeer i. 307 (page 434)]

Journal entry: Calais — [Lough]

Friday, 29 Nov. 1675, n.s.

19 Nov. 1675, o.s.

“Went to Boulogne, 7 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Saturday, 30 Nov. 1675, n.s.

20 Nov. 1675, o.s.

“To Montreuil, 7 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

  ▲  

December

Sunday, 1 Dec. 1675, n.s.

21 Nov. 1675, o.s.

“To Abbeville, 10 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Monday, 2 Dec. 1675, n.s.

22 Nov. 1675, o.s.

“To Poix, 9 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Tuesday, 3 Dec. 1675, n.s.

23 Nov. 1675, o.s.

“To Beauvais, 9 leagues, and thence to Tillart, 3 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Wednesday, 4 Dec. 1675, n.s.

24 Nov. 1675, o.s.

“To Beaumont, 5 leagues, thence to Paris, 8 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 1]

Journal entries (covers 4-13 December): Cariage, Portage, Vectura, Diging [i.e. Digging], Invalides, Pension, Cough, Tussis — [Lough, Dewhurst]

Thursday, 5 Dec. 1675, n.s.

25 Nov. 1675, o.s.

At Paris.

Letter dated: From Thomas Stringer. — [De Beer, i. 307]

Thursday, 12 Dec. 1675, n.s.

2 Dec. 1675, o.s.

Locke obtains a letter of credit from E. Margas in Paris, to Simon Peloutier at Lyons, for up to 976 livres, in exchange for Locke’s bills of exchange on London. — MS Locke c. 15, ff. 224-225.

Saturday, 14 Dec. 1675, n.s.

4 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We took coach at Paris in the diligence for Lyons and, after having dined at Melun, got by an hour or 2 in the night to Moret where we lodged. It is 16 leagues from Paris.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 2]

Journal entries: Melun, Moret — [Lough]

Sunday, 15 Dec. 1675, n.s.

5 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“Dind at Pont sur Yonne & reached Joyny [Joigny], 17 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 2]

Journal entry: Joyny [i.e. Joigny] — [Lough]

Monday, 16 Dec. 1675, n.s.

6 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We dined at Auxerre … We lay at Lacie le Bois [Lucy-le-Bois], 15 leagues …” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 2]

Journal entries: Auxerre, Lacie le Boys [i.e. Lucy-le-Bois] — [Lough]

Tuesday, 17 Dec. 1675, n.s.

7 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We lay at Arnan le Duc [Arnay-le-Duc], 15 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 3]

Journal entries: Arnan le Dec [i.e. Arnay-le-Duc], Lignum, Oxen — [Lough]

Wednesday, 18 Dec. 1675, n.s.

8 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We dind at a litle town cald Chany [Chagny], 8 Leagues … Lay at Challon [Châlons-sur-Saône], 3 leagues …” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 3]

Journal entries: Chany [i.e. Chagny], Challon [i.e. Châlons-sur-Saône] — [Lough]

Thursday, 19 Dec. 1675, n.s.

9 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We took boat at Challon on the Saone … We passed by Tornus [Tournus] … 5 Leagues from Tornus we came to Mascon where we lay.” — [Journal 1675-76, pp. 4-5]

Journal entries: Turnus [i.e. Tournus], Abbatium, Mascon — [Lough]

Friday, 20 Dec. 1675, n.s.

10 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We enterd our boat again and at 5 leagues’s distance came to a pretty towne calld Bel-Ville … A league from Belville we came to Ville Franche … Two Leagues farther we came to Trevou [Trévoux] … From thence to Lyons, 3 leagues …” — [Journal 1675-76, pp. 5-6]

Journal entries: Belville, Ville Franque, Trevou, Baujolais [i.e. Beaujolais], Vectura, Portage — [Lough]

Saturday, 21 Dec. 1675, n.s.

11 Dec. 1675, o.s.

In Lyon.

Journal entry: Lyon — [Lough]

Locke met William Charleton and travelled with him to Montpellier.

Sunday, 22 Dec. 1675, n.s.

12 Dec. 1675, o.s.

In Lyon.

Journal entries: Jesuits’ Colledg, Mr. Servir Musaeum — [Lough]

Monday, 23 Dec. 1675, n.s.

13 Dec. 1675, o.s.

In Lyon.

Journal entries: St. John’s, Clock, Horologium — [Lough]

Tuesday, 24 Dec. 1675, n.s.

14 Dec. 1675, o.s.

In Lyon.

Journal entries: Pere encise [i.e. Pierre-Scize], Maison de ville — [Lough]

Wednesday, 25 Dec. 1675, n.s.

15 Dec. 1675, o.s.

In Lyon.

Journal entries: Hostel de Dieu, Nosodochium, Ptochodochium, Charité — [Lough]

Thursday, 26 Dec. 1675, n.s.

16 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“[W]e took horse & rode to Vienne, 5 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 17]

Journal entries: Ptochodochium, Granary, Horreum, Vienne, Passer solitarius — [Lough]

Friday, 27 Dec. 1675, n.s.

17 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We went 7 leagues to St. Valer [Saint-Vallier] …” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 18]

Journal entry: St. Valer — [Lough]

Saturday, 28 Dec. 1675, n.s.

18 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“To Valence, 7 leagues …” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 18]

Journal entries: Valence, Hermitage, Thuin — [Lough]

Sunday, 29 Dec. 1675, n.s.

19 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“To Montelimar, 8 leagues.” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 19]

Journal entry: Montlimar — [Lough]

Monday, 30 Dec. 1675, n.s.

20 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“From Montelimar to Pon[t] St. Esprit, 5 leagues [via Chasteauneuf and Pallu [Lapalud]]. 3 leagues from Pont St. Esprit we came to Orange …” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 19-22]

Journal entries: Pont St. Esprit, Chasteauneuf, Husbandry, Agricultura, Pilati domus, Chasteau Neuf, Pallu, Pont St. Esprit, Orange, Bombycina, Silkwindeing — [Lough]

Tuesday, 31 Dec. 1675, n.s.

21 Dec. 1675, o.s.

“We came to Avignon, 4 leagues from Orange …” — [Journal 1675-76, p. 24]

Journal entry: Avignon [twice] — [Lough]
